The short version. HogFarm itself sends zero emails — it just calls PostHog's provisioning API, and PostHog (via Customer.io) sends everything.
  1. "Claim your account" email — this is your set-password email. There is no separate one. The subject literally says "Welcome to PostHog – Set your password," and the Claim your account button links to the password-reset page. Clicking it is how you set a password and log in. Nothing is missing — the wording just split "claim" (button) from "password" (subject), so it wasn't obvious.
  2. Wizard email — you're right, this should not fire for a provisioned account. It's the first email of PostHog's 41-email self-serve onboarding drip ("Onboarding 9.0"). Provisioning trips it accidentally because the drip's only exclusion is "self-hosted users" — it never excludes partner-provisioned users.

How provisioning decides what to send

Provisioning branches on whether the email already has a PostHog account. The red path is the accidental enrollment into the self-serve wizard drip.

Entry point: POST /api/agentic/provisioning/account_requests (ee/api/agentic_provisioning/views.py:288). It routes to one of two handlers:

  • New email → _handle_new_user (views.py:678): creates the user with password=None, fires the "user signed up" analytics event, and queues send_provisioning_welcome → Email 67.
  • Existing PostHog account → _handle_existing_user (views.py:496): browser consent (or silent if the partner is trusted), and sends a one-time link code → Email 69. No new user is created, so the wizard drip is not triggered on this path.

Why the wizard email leaks through

"Onboarding 9.0" (campaign 161) is an event-triggered drip. Its entry rule, decoded from the live config:

trigger event : "user signed up"
filter        : NOT in segment 16   ("Self-hosted Users" — users not on Cloud)
subscription  : topic 2
The exclusion list contains exactly one item, and it's the wrong concern. The only thing that keeps a user out of the wizard drip is being self-hosted. A partner-provisioned Cloud account fires the same "user signed up" event as a self-serve signup, isn't self-hosted, and therefore enters the drip. The provisioning path already carries everything needed to tell them apart — it just isn't used as a filter:
  • signup_backend_processor = "AgenticProvisioning" on the event (event_usage.py:52)
  • is_email_verified = NULL and has_password_set = false on the person

Recommendations

  1. Fix the wizard leak (the real bug). Add a negated exclusion to campaign 161's entry filter for provisioned users — define a Customer.io segment on signup_backend_processor = "AgenticProvisioning" and negate it, exactly the way "Self-hosted Users" (segment 16) is already negated. Mirrors the existing pattern, no backend change needed.
  2. Decide who owns the wizard pitch. The claim email and the wizard drip both push npx @posthog/wizard@latest. For provisioned users the claim email should own it; suppressing the drip (step 1) resolves the duplication.
  3. Tighten the claim email's framing (the perceived bug). Nothing is broken — but subject "Set your password" vs button "Claim your account" reads as two different things. Align them, e.g. button "Claim your account & set password", so users don't wait for a second email that never comes.
